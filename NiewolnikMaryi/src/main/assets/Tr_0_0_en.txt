<h3> Matthew 5, 1-16 </h3>
<br/>
<b>CHRIST PREACHES SEVEN BLESSINGS.</b>
<br/>
And when Jesus saw the crowds, he went up into a mountain, and when he sat down, his disciples came to him. And opening his mouth, he taught them, saying, Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven. Blessed are the meek, for they will inherit the earth. Blessed are those who weep, for they will be comforted. Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they will be filled. Blessed are the merciful, for they will obtain mercy.
Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God. Blessed are the peacemakers, for they will be called sons of God. Blessed are those who are persecuted for righteousness' sake, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven. Blessed are you when they curse you and persecute you and say all kinds of evil against you, lying, for my sake. Rejoice and be glad, for great is your reward in heaven: for so they persecuted the prophets who were before you.<br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
<b>HE CALLS THE APOSTLES SALT AND LIGHT.</b>
<br/>
<br/>
You are the salt of the earth. And if the salt becomes stale, what will it be salted with? It is of no further use than to be thrown out and trampled under foot by men. You are the light of the world. A city on a mountain cannot be hidden. Neither do they light a candle and put it under a basket, but on a stand, and it gives light to all who are in the house. Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven.
<br/>
<br/>
<h3> From the book "On the Imitation of Christ, Book III, Chapter 40</h3>
<br/>
1. The student. Lord, what is man, that you remember him, and what is the son of man, that you take care of him? (Cf. Mt 26:41). What has a man done to deserve that you should grant him graces? How can I complain if you leave me, or complain that you do not do what I ask? In fact, I can only think and say one thing: Lord, I am nothing, I can do nothing, and of myself I have nothing good; I feel my weakness and nothingness in everything, and I am always heading towards nothingness. If You do not support me and strengthen me internally, I immediately become cold and sluggish.<br> </br>
2. But You, Lord, are always the same and remain eternally good, just and holy; You do all things well, justly and holy, and dispose of all things with wisdom. And I, being more inclined to fall than to advance, never remain in the same state; the moods in my soul change seven times. However, if you feel like extending a helping hand to me, things will quickly get better. You alone, without human help, can support me and confirm me so strongly that I will not be so changeable, but my heart, turned to You, will find peace in You.<br> </br>

<h3>Veni, Creator Spiritus</h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, descend, visit the hearts of your faithful circle, send heavenly grace to the breasts that are the work of your hands.<br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, a gift of Almighty God, you are the anointing of our souls, a living source, love, fire.<br> </br>
3. You give grace sevenfold, because you have power from the right hand of the Father, promised to us by the Father, you enrich our language with your speech.<br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, and strengthen the fragile weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your power.<br> </br>
5. Cast out the enemy and give you peace forever. May all harm pass away from us under your persistent leadership.<br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, and let us, with the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our strength.<br> </br>
7. Glory and honor be to God the Father, to the Son who rose from the dead, and to Him who comforts us, for eternity, which has no end.<br> </br>
Amen.

<h3>Ave, Maris Stella</h3>
<br/>
<b>Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J.</b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hello, fiery Star of the seas, Mother of God, ever-pure Virgin, blissful gate of heaven.
Gabriella: "Ave" being greeted, return us gracious days, calling us Eve. Remove the chains from prisoners, light fires for the blind, take away the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, protect God in heaven who, out of great love, wanted to be born from you. O strange lady, silent before everyone, break the sins, make us silent.
Give us a pure life, a path that does not deceive, so that when we see the Child we will always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, and let one eternal worship burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Allow me to praise You, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against Your enemies!

<h3>Magnificat - Song of Thanksgiving to the Blessed Virgin Mary</h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowliness of his handmaid;
For from now on all nations will call me blessed. For he who is mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from generation to generation to those who fear him.
He made mighty with his arm, and scattered the proud in the imagination of their hearts. He has brought down the mighty from their capital and raised up the humble.
He filled the hungry with good things and left the rich empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and to his descendants forever.
