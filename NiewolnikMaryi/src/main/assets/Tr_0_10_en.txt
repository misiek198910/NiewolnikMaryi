<h3> On following Christ, Fr. I, Chap. 25 </h3>
<br/>
2. Someone who is restless has often been carried away by fear, then by hope; once, tormented by distress, he entered the church and, falling on his face in front of one of the altars, prayed and troubled himself, saying: Oh, if I knew that I would endure! Immediately he heard God's answer in his heart: And if you knew this, what would you do? Do now what you would like to do then and you will be completely safe. Consoled immediately and strengthened, he moved to the Divine Will, and he was established by his tormenting uncertainty. He was no longer curious about his future, but both when starting and doing each good cause, he only asked what God's Will was and what God might like. <br> </br>
3. Hope in the Lord and do good, says the Prophet, and dwell in the land, and you will feed on its riches. (Ps 36: 3). There is one thing that distracts many from improvement and zealous improvement: the fear of difficulties and the toil of struggle. After all, in comparison with others, those above all walk in virtue, who fight the harder the more difficulties and contradictions they encounter. Man is perfected the most and gains more grace when he overcomes and humiliates himself the most. <br> </br>
4. But not all have as much to overcome and to mortification. A person who is keen on competing, even though he has many passions, will progress faster than someone of good disposition but less zeal. Two things are especially useful for profound improvement: to break away by force from our inborn evil inclinations and to earnestly pursue the virtues we need most. You should also try to avoid it in particular and fight in yourself what you don't like about others. <br> </br>
5. Use everything for your improvement; if you see or hear of good examples, be eager to follow them. If, however, you see something reprehensible, beware that you do not do it yourself, and if you do, try to correct it as quickly as possible. As your eye looks at others, so does others look at you. How nice and sweet to see brothers who are fervent, pious, submissive and punitive! How sad and sad it is to see those who are distracted and who do not fulfill their calling! Oh, how harmful it is to neglect your vocation, and to lean towards what we are not told! <br> </br>
6. Remember the arrangement you have made, and keep Christ crucified before your eyes. Looking at the life of Christ, you are ashamed that you have tried so little to follow it, even though you have been on God's path for so long ... If Jesus Crucified had entered our heart, how quickly would we have acquired perfect wisdom. <br> </br>

<h3> Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
