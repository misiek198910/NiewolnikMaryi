<h3> St. Luke 11: 1-13 </h3>
<br/>
PERSISTENCE IN PRAYER.
<br/>
<br/>
And he said to them, Who among you will have a friend, and go to him at midnight and say to him, "Friend, lend me three loaves; because my friend has come to me from the way, and I have nothing to put in front of him. " And he from within, answering, would say, “Don't bother me, the door is already locked, and my children are in bed with me; I can't get up and give you. " And if he continues to knock, I tell you: although he will not give him and will not get up, because he is his friend, he will get up to be obtrusive and give him as much as he needs. And I say to you: Ask, and it will be given you; seek and you will find; knock and it will be opened to you. For everyone who asks receives; and whoever seeks finds; to the knocker it will be opened. Who among you, if he asks his father for bread, will give him a stone; or for a fish, will he give him a snake instead of a fish? Or if he asked for an egg, would she give him a scorpion? If you then, although evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your Father from heaven give a good spirit to those who ask him? <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Fr. I, Chap. 25 </h3>
<br/>
9. May we do nothing but praise the Lord our God with our heart and mouth. Oh, if you never needed to eat or drink, but could still worship him and devote yourself only to the things of the spirit! Then you would be much happier than now when you have to serve your body in its every need. May there be no such needs, but only spiritual food for the soul, in which, unfortunately, we taste so rarely! <br> </br>
10. When man realizes that he no longer seeks consolation in every creature, he only begins to love God perfectly; and then he will always be perfectly content, however things turn out. Then neither good luck will make him too happy, nor will adversity sadden him, and he will surrender himself with complete trust to God, who for him is all in everything and for whom nothing is lost, nothing dies, but all lives and serves him at his beck and call. <br> </br>
11. Always remember the end and the fact that lost time does not come back. You will never acquire virtue without zeal and diligence. When you start to get cold, it is bad for you. If you devote yourself entirely to earnest service, then you will find peace, and by the love of virtue and by God's grace, you learn that this work is not so hard. A passionate and diligent person is ready for anything. It takes more effort to overcome faults and passions than to bear the hardest hardships and hardships of manual labor. A drunkard worker will not get rich, and whoever despises little things will slowly fall. (Ekli 19,1) If you spend the day profitably, you will be glad in the evening. Watch yourself, give yourself a stimulus, exhort yourself, and however others act, do not neglect yourself. You will only improve, as long as you control yourself. <br> </br>

<h3> Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
