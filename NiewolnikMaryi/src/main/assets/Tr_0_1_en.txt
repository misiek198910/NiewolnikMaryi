<h3> St. Matthew 5, 17-26 </h3>
<br/>
CAME TO PERFECT THE ORDER.
<br/>
<br/>
Do not suppose that I am come to break the law or the prophets. I have not come to solve, but to fill. For truly I say to you, until heaven and earth pass away, one iota or one dash will not be changed in the law, until all is done. Whoever, then, looses one of these least commandments, and so teaches people, will be called least in the kingdom of heaven; and whoever obeys and teaches will be called great in the kingdom of heaven. For I tell you, unless your righteousness abounds more than the religious doctors and Pharisees, you will not enter the kingdom of heaven. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
HE TEACHES NOT TO BE ANGRY AT HIS NEIGHBOR.
<br/>
<br/>
You have heard that the old man said, "Thou shalt not kill," and whoever kills will be guilty of judgment. But I tell you that anyone who is angry with his brother will be guilty of judgment. But whoever says to his brother, you fool, will be guilty of the Council. And whoever says: mad will be guilty of the fire of hell. <br> </br>
If therefore you offer your gift to the altar and there you remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there in front of the altar, and first go be reconciled to your brother: and then come and offer your gift. Make peace with your adversary, while you are on the way with him, lest your adversary hand you over to the judge, and the judge hand you over to the servant, and you will be thrown into prison. Truly I say to you, you will not get out until you have given the last penny.

<h3> On following Christ, Book III, Ch. 40 </h3>
<br/>
3.If I could, therefore, get rid of all human consolation, be it for godliness or a need that urges me to seek You (because not for a man to comfort me), then I could rightly expect everything from Your grace and to enjoy the gift of a new consolation. <br> </br>
4. Thanks be to you, from whom everything comes, whatever good happens to me. I am a fickle and weak man, a vanity and nothing to You. So what can I boast of or why do I crave publicity? I guess I'm nothing, and that would be utter vanity. Indeed, vain glory is a fatal disease and the greatest vanity, because it distracts from true glory and deprives heavenly grace. When a man pleases himself, he displeases you, when he pursues human praise, he loses his true virtues. <br> </br>
5. The real glory and holy joy is to boast of you, not to yourself, to be glad for your name and not for your own virtue, not to enjoy any creature except for you. Praised be your name, not mine; Let your works be glorified, not mine, let your name be blessed, and let me have no human glory. You alone are my glory and the joy of my heart. In You I will boast and rejoice all day long, and I will boast of nothing but my weaknesses. (2 Cor 12: 5) <br> </br>

<h3> Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
