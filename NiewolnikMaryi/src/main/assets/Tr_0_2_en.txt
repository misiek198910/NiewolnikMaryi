<h3> St. Matthew 5, 27-37 </h3>
<br/>
OF JUSTICE, WORSHIP, AND MIRACLESS
<br/>
<br/>
You have heard that the old people were told, You shall not commit adultery. " And I say to you that everyone who looks at a woman to desire her has already made her happy in his heart. If, then, your right eye stumbles you, tear it out and throw it away, for it is better for you to lose one of your members than for your whole body to be thrown into hell. And if your right hand stumbles you, cut it off and throw it away; for it is better for you that one of your members should perish than for your whole body to go to Hell. It was said, "Whoever divorces his wife, let him give her a certificate of divorce." But I say to you that whoever divorces his wife commits her adultery, and whoever accepts the abandoned commits adultery. <br> </br>

ABOUT THE SWORN
<br/>
<br/>
You have also heard that the old people were told, “You shall not perjure; but you will give your oaths to the Lord. " But I say to you, do not swear at all: neither by heaven, because it is God's throne, nor by earth, because it is his footstool, nor by Jerusalem, because it is the city of the great king. Neither shall you swear by your head, for you cannot make one hair white or black. But let your speech be: yes, yes; no no. And what's more, is than evil. <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Fr. I, Chap. 18 </h3>
<br/>
1. Look at the living examples of the Holy Fathers who shone with true perfection and piety, and you will see how small and almost nothing what we do. Ah, what is our life compared to theirs! The saints and friends of Christ served the Lord in hunger and thirst, in cold and nakedness, in work and toil, in vigil and fasting, in prayer and holy meditation, in persecution and insults. How many and dire afflictions were suffered by the apostles, martyrs, followers, virgins and all others who wanted to follow in Christ's footsteps! For whoever hates his soul in this world will keep it for eternal life. (Jn 12:25) <br> </br>
2. Oh, how harsh and denied life the holy Fathers lived in the desert! How long and hard they endured the temptations! How often were they tormented by the enemy! How many fervent prayers they have prayed to God! How sharp the posts were! <br> </br>
With what zeal and zeal they strove for spiritual progress! How fierce they fought to overcome their faults. With what a pure and simple thought they always turned to God! They worked during the day and at night they prayed for a long time, although they did not stop praying mentally during the classes. <br> </br>
3. Each moment was usefully used, and each moment devoted to God's service seemed short to them. For the great sweetness of contemplation, they even forgot the need for a body meal. They renounced all riches, dignities, honors, friends and relatives; they wanted nothing from the world, accepting only the things necessary for life and lamenting that they had to serve the body. They were therefore poor in earthly things, but very rich in graces and virtues. Outwardly deprived of everything, they received inwardly God's grace and consolation. <br> </br>

<h3>Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
