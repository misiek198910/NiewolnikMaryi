<h3> St. Matthew 6, 1-23 </h3>
<br/>
HOW TO PERFORM GOOD WORKS, ALMOND?
<br/>
<br/>
Take heed that you do not do your righteousness before men, that you may be seen from them; otherwise you will have no reward with your Father which is in heaven. So when you give alms, do not trumpet in front of you, as the hypocrites do in the synagogues and in the streets, that they may be worshiped by the people. Truly I say to you, they have received their wages. But when you give alms, do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing, that your alms may be in a secret place. and your Father who sees in secret will reward you. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
HOW TO PRAY?
<br/>
<br/>
And when you pray, do not be like the hypocrites who pray in the synagogues and standing on the street corners to be seen by the people. Truly I say to you, they have received their wages. But you, when you pray, go into your closet, and shut the door and pray to your Father in secret; and your Father who sees in secret will reward you. But when you pray, do not speak much, as the heathen do; for they think that they will be heard in their many words. So do not be like them; for your Father knows what you need before you ask him. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
THE LORD'S PRAYER.
<br/>
<br/>
Then you will pray this way: Our Father who art in heaven! Hallowed be thy name. Your kingdom come. Thy will be done, as it is in heaven also on earth. Give us today our daily bread. And forgive us our debts as we forgive those who trespass against us. And lead us not into temptation. But save us from evil. Amen. For if you forgive others their sins, your heavenly Father will also forgive your sins. But if you do not forgive people, neither will your Father forgive your sins. <br> </br>
<br/>
HOW TO FAST?
<br/>
<br/>
But when you fast, do not be sad like the hypocrites; for they wear out their faces to show people that they are fasting. Truly I say to you that they have received their wages. But you who fast, anoint your head and wash your face, lest you show yourself to men that you are fasting, but to your Father who is in secret; and your Father who sees in secret will reward you. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
ABOUT THE TREASURY OF HEAVEN
<br/>
<br/>
Do not treasure yourselves in the land where rust and moth spoil and where thieves dig up and steal. But treasure yourselves treasures in heaven, where neither rust nor moth can spoil, and where thieves do not dig up or steal. For where your treasure is, there is your heart also. Your eye is the shine of your body. If your eye is sincere, all your body will be light. But if your eye is wicked, your whole body will be dark. So if the light in you is darkness, how great will the darkness itself be? <br> </br>

<h3> Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
