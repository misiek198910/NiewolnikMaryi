<h3> St. Matthew 6,24-34 </h3>
<br/>
ON TRUST IN GOD
<br/>
<br/>
No one can serve two masters; for either he will hate the one and love the other, or else he will stand by the one and despise the other. You cannot serve God and mammon. Therefore I tell you: do not be anxious about your life, what you will eat, nor about your body with what you will wear. Isn't life more important than food and the body more than clothing? Look at the birds of the sky, that they neither sow nor reap, and that they are not gathered in barns, and your heavenly Father feeds them. Are you not far more important than they are? And which of you, by thinking, can add one cubit to his height? And why do you care about clothing? See how the lilies of the field grow: they neither work nor spin. But I tell you that neither Solomon in all his glory was clothed like one of these. But if the grass of the field, which is today, and tomorrow will be thrown into the oven, God clothe it, how much more of you, little faith? Therefore do not be anxious, saying, What shall we eat, or what shall we drink, or what shall we put on? Because the pagans are urgently looking for it. For your heavenly Father knows you need all these things. Seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things will be given you. So don't worry about tomorrow; for tomorrow will take care of itself. The day has enough of its misery. <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Fr. III, Ch. 10 </h3>
<br/>
1. Student. Lord! Here I am turning to you again, I cannot keep silent; I will speak to the Lord my God and the King of heaven. How much is your sweetness, Lord, which you have kept for those who fear you. (Ps 30:20). And what are you to those who love You and who serve You wholeheartedly? You bestow inexpressible sweetness to those who think about you and make those who love you happy. You showed your unlimited love to me, first of all, by creating me when I did not exist, and when I wandered away from You, You drew me to You to serve You, and You wanted me to love You. <br> </br>
2. Source of eternal love, what shall I say about you? Could I forget you when you deigned to remember me, even when I was wasting away and dying? You took pity on your servant beyond all expectations and showed me grace and friendship undeservedly. How can I repay you for that? For not everyone is given to do everything <br> </br>
having renounced, they forsook the world and dedicated themselves to religious life. What is it that I serve you when all creation should serve you? I cannot consider God's service to be something great; rather, it seems great and wonderful that you wanted to accept me, so unworthy and wretched, as a servant and join the group of Your beloved ones. <br> </br>
3. Yours is everything I have and what I serve you. Yes, you serve me more than I serve you. Behold, heaven and earth, which you made for man, serve me and carry out all your commands daily. And that's not enough: you wanted even angels to serve people. Everything, however, is overcome by the fact that you, Lord, deign to serve man and you promised yourself to give him. <br> </br>

<h3> Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
