<h3> St. Matthew 7, 1-14 </h3>
<br/>
DO NOT JUDGE.
<br/>
<br/>
Judge not, lest you be judged. For with what kind of judgment will you judge you, they will judge you, and with what measure you will measure, they will measure you. Why do you see the speck in your brother's eye, and not the log in your own eye? Or how you say to your brother, Let me take the stalk out of your eye, and, behold, the log is in your eye. You hypocrite! First throw the log out of your own eye, and then you will see how to throw the speck out of your brother's eye. Do not give the dogs what is holy, nor throw your pearls in front of the hogs, lest they accidentally trample them with their feet, and turn around, lest they tear you apart. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
RECOMMENDS PRAYER.
<br/>
<br/>
Ask and it will be given to you; seek and you will find; knock and it will be opened to you. For everyone who asks receives; and whoever seeks finds; and to a knocker it will be opened. Or which of you, if his son asks for bread, will give him a stone? Or if he asks for a fish, will give him a snake? If you then, although you are evil, know how to give good gifts to your sons, how much more will your Father who is in the heavens give good things to those who ask him? However, whatever you want the people to do to you, do them also; for it is the law and the prophets. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
ON A NARROW AND WIDE ROAD.
<br/>
<br/>
Enter through the narrow gate; for the gate is wide and the way is broad that leads to destruction, and there are many who enter by it. Oh, how narrow is the gate and how narrow is the road that leads to life, and few find it! <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Fr. III, Ch. 10 </h3>
<br/>
4. What will I give you for all these goods? May I serve you all the days of my life! If only for one day I could serve you with dignity! Indeed, You alone are worthy of all service, honor and everlasting glory. Indeed, you are my Lord, and I am your poor servant, who should serve You with all my strength and never rest in praise of You. I want it so and I want it so, and what I don't get, you have to complete. <br> </br>
5. It is a great honor and glory to serve You and to give up everything else for your sake. Those who voluntarily engage themselves in Your most sacred service will obtain abundant grace. All those who, for your love, have renounced all pleasure of the flesh, will experience the sweetest consolation of the Holy Spirit. Great freedom of thought will be achieved by those who, for your sake, follow the narrow path and get rid of all worldly concerns. <br> </br>
6. For the grateful and joyful service of God through which man becomes truly free and holy. About the holy state of religious life, which makes man equal to angels, pleasing to God, terrible-satans, worthy of respect by all believers. Dear and welcome service! Through you the highest good is served and eternal joy is obtained! <br> </br>

<h3> Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
