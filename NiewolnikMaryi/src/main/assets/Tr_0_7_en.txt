<h3> St. Matthew 7, 15-29 </h3>
<br/>
ABOUT TRULY GOOD WORKERS
<br/>
<br/>
Beware of the false prophets who come to you in sheep's clothing and inside they are ravenous wolves. You will know them by their fruits. Do they pick berries from thorns <br> </br>
wine or thistle figs? So every good tree bears good fruit, and a bad tree bears bad fruit. A good tree cannot bear bad fruit, nor can a bad tree bear good fruit. Any tree that does not bear good fruit will be cut down and thrown into the fire. Therefore you will know them by their fruits. Not everyone who says: Lord, Lord, will enter the kingdom of heaven, but the one who does the will of my Father who is in heaven will enter the kingdom of heaven. Many will say to me on that day: Lord, Lord! did we not prophesy in thy name, and cast out demons in thy name, and work many miracles in thy name. And then I will confess to them: I never knew you; depart from me, you workers of iniquity. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
ABOUT STRONG BUILDING
<br/>
<br/>
Everyone who hears these words of mine and does them will be like a wise man who built his house on a rock. And the rain fell, and the rivers came, and the winds blew, and struck that house, and it did not fall; for he was founded on a rock. And everyone who hears these words of mine and does not obey them will be like a foolish man who built his house on sand. And the rain fell, and the rivers came, and the winds blew, and struck that house; and he fell, and great was his fall. It happened, after Jesus had finished saying these words, that the multitude was amazed at his teaching. For he taught them as having authority, and not as their doctors and Pharisees. <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Book III, Ch. 7 </h3>

4. Whoever feels very safe in times of peace will be too frightened and wavered in combat. If you could be humble and modest at all times, and prudently guide and govern your spirit, you would not be so easily exposed to the danger of sin. It is good advice, while feeling spiritual fervor within you, think about what will happen when God's light goes out. When you experience the lack of light again, think that the grace I have taken away from you for the time being for my glory and for your warning may return. <br> </br>
5. Often times, trying to do this is more useful than if you were always successful. Merit should not be judged by whether one has much consolation and revelation or is proficient in knowledge of the Scriptures. or whether he is in a high position, but whether he is grounded in true humility and filled with God's love, whether he sincerely and in everything he seeks God's glory, considers himself nothing and truly indulges himself, and whether he can rejoice more when he is humiliated and despised than revered. <br> </br>

<h3> Veni, Creator Spiritus </h3>
<br/>
1. O Creator, Spirit, come down, lead the hearts of the faithful to You circle, send Heavenly grace to your breasts, which are the work of your hands. <br> </br>
2. You are called the Comforter, You are the gift of Almighty God, You are the anointing of our souls, the living spring, love, ardor of fire. <br> </br>
3. You are grace sevenfold, for you have strength from the right hand of the Father, promised by the Father, you enrich our language with speech. <br> </br>
4. Illuminate our thoughts with light, pour holy love into our hearts, And confirm the weak weakness of our bodies with the constancy of your strength. <br> </br>
5. Drive the enemy out, and give peace for everlasting time. Under your steadfast leadership, let all harm be avoided. <br> </br>
6. Let us know the Father through You, let the Son also be known, And let us, the Spirit of both of them, believe in You with all our might. <br> </br>
7. Be to God the Father, glory, honor, the Son who raised from the dead, And to the One who comforts us, for eternity whose end is a stranger. <br> </br>
Amen.

<h3> Ave, Maris Stella </h3>
<b> Hymn from the 7th or 8th century, translated from Latin by Fr. Karłowski, K.J. </b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail sea, fiery Star, Mother of God, Virgin always pure, heaven's gate blessed.
Gabryela: “Ave” being welcomed, return to us kind days, changing the name of Eve. Take off the fetters of prisoners, light the fires of the blind, remove the pain of life, ask for all the gifts.
Be our Mother, defend God in heaven, who wanted to be born of you through great love. Virgin peculiar, meek before all, break the links of sins, make us meek.
Give us a clean life, an honest way, so that when we see the Child, we may always be happy.
Glory be to God the Father, worship the power of Christ, let one eternal honor burn with the Three Spirit! Amen.
Let me praise you, Holy Virgin! Give me strength against your enemies!

<h3> Magnificat - Hymn of thanksgiving of the Blessed Virgin Mary </h3>
<br/>
My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior. For he has considered the lowness of his handmaid;
For, from now on, all nations will call me blessed. Mighty has done great things for me, and holy is his name.
And his mercy is from all to all those who fear him.
He has made strength his arm, he has scattered the proud in their hearts. He threw down the mighty from the capital and raised up the lowly.
He has filled the hungry with good, and let the rich go empty.
He welcomed Israel his servant, remembering his mercy.
As he spoke to our fathers, to Abraham and his descendants for ever.
