<h3> Treatise on the perfect devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary, Cz. II, Ch. 3 </h3>
<br/>
Our best deeds are usually tainted and tainted by the evil we have from our contaminated nature. When pure, transparent water is poured into a stinking vessel, or wine is poured into a barrel whose interior is not clean, clean water and good wine deteriorate and are easily saturated with an unpleasant odor. The same thing happens when God puts the dew of heaven of grace or the marvelous wine of His love inside our soul, polluted by original and works sin. Well, His gifts usually deteriorate and become stained as a result of the evil seed that sin has left in us; Our works, even the most sublime virtues, are infected with it. So, to attain perfection, which is impossible to attain without communion with Jesus Christ, it is very important to cast out what is bad in us; otherwise our Lord, who is infinitely pure and does not bear the slightest blemish in the soul, will throw us away from His face and will not be united with us.
To let go of ourselves, we need:
With the help of the light of the Holy Spirit to know well our defiled nature, our ineptitude in all that is good, our weakness in all things, our continual inconstancy, our incompatibility of all grace and our general iniquity. The sin of our first parent poisoned us all, acidified and spoiled, as the acid acidifies and spoils the dough into which it is put. The sins that we have committed, whether mortal or venial, although already forgiven, have increased our lust, weakness, instability and our corruption, leaving bad consequences and consequences in the soul.
Our bodies are so corrupt that the Holy Spirit. he calls them the bodies of sin (Rom 6: 6), conceived in sin
(Ps 50: 7), fed in sin and capable only of sin. They are subject to thousands of diseases, becoming weaker day by day, until they are preyed by worms and rot.
Our soul, connected with the body, became so sensual that it was called flesh because all flesh contaminated its path on earth. (Genesis 6:12). Our participation is nothing but pride and the blindness of the spirit, hardness of the heart, weakness and instability of the soul, sensuality of rebellious passions and diseases of the body. By nature we are more proud than peacocks, more attached to the ground than toads, wicked than goats, more jealous than serpents, more swift than tigers, lazier than turtles, weaker than reeds, more changeable than flags on the roof. We only have nothingness and sin of ourselves, and we deserve only God's sin and eternal hell.
Is it any wonder then that the Savior said that whoever wants to follow Him should deny himself and despise his own soul? (Cf. Lk 9:23; Mat 16:24). Whoever loves his soul will lose it; and whoever hates his soul in this world will keep it for eternal life. (Jn 12:25). Infinite Wisdom, which does not give commandments without a reason, makes us hate ourselves because we deserve hate so much: nothing is as worthy of love as God, nothing is as worthy of hatred as ourselves.
