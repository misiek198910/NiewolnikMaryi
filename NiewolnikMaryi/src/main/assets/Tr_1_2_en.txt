<h3> St. Luke 13: 1-9 </h3>
<br/>
A REMINDER FOR PENANCE.
<br/>
<br/>
Some came at that time, telling him of the Galileans, whose blood Pilate had mixed with their sacrifices. And answering, he said to them, Do you suppose these Galileans were more sinful than all the other Galileans, that they suffered such things? No, I tell you; but if you do not repent, you will all perish likewise. Like the eighteen on whom the tower of Siloah fell and killed them, do you suppose that they too were guilty of more than all the people of Jerusalem? No, I'm telling you; but if you do not repent, you will all die likewise. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
THE PARABLE OF FIDE.
<br/>
<br/>
And he said this parable. A certain man had a fig tree planted in his vineyard, and he came looking for fruit and found none. And he said to the grower of the vine, Behold, it is three years when I come to look for fruit in this pony, and find none; cut it down, why does it take up the earth? And he answering said to him, Lord, leave her for this year, and I will dig her up and cover her with dung, and perhaps she will bear fruit. and if not, then you will cut it out. <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Book III, Ch. 13 </h3>
<br/>
1. Christ. The son, who tries to decline from obedience, withdraws from grace, and whoever seeks personal gain loses the common good. Whoever reluctantly and involuntarily submits to his superior proves that his body is not yet perfectly obedient to him, but often rebels and murmurs. So learn to submit to your boss more willingly if you want to tame your body. For it is easier to defeat the external enemy who is not divided inside. You have no worse and more dangerous enemy of your soul than yourself, when the body in you does not agree with the spirit. You must consider yourself absolutely nothing if you want to overcome flesh and blood. You still love yourself in a disorderly manner, and therefore you shudder from completely surrendering yourself to someone else's will. <br> </br>
2. What is it that you submit to man for God, you who are dust and nothing, since I, the Almighty and the Most High, who made everything out of nothing, have been humbly subject to man for you? I have become the humblest and lowest of all so that you may overcome your pride with my humility. Learn to be obedient, dust! Learn to root, earth and clay, and bow to everyone's feet. Learn to overcome your cravings and obey completely. <br> </br>
3. Burn hard against yourself and do not suffer from pride ruling over you; become so docile and small that all may walk on you and trample you like the mud on the road. Why should you complain, you vain man? How can you contradict those who throw anger in your eyes, the wicked sinner who has offended God so many times and deserved hell? My eyes pity you, for your soul was dear before me; I want you to know my love and be always grateful for my favors; that you may remain truly submissive and humble and endure even contempt with patience. <br> </br>
