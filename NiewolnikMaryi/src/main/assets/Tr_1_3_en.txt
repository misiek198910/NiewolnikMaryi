<h3> St. Luke 13: 10-21 </h3>
<br/>
THE WOMAN IS HEALED ON THE SHABBATH
<br/>
<br/>
And he taught them in the synagogue on the Sabbaths. And behold, there was a woman who had a spirit of infirmity for eighteen years, and was bent over, and could not look up by any means. When Jesus saw her, he called her to him and said to her, Woman, you are freed from your infirmity. And he put his hands on her, and immediately she straightened up and praised God. The leader of the synagogue, in reply, indignant that Jesus had healed on the Sabbath, said to the crowd: There are six days when work should be done, so come and be healed on those days, not on a Saturday. And the Lord answered him, You hypocrites! Does not each of you on the Sabbath untie his ox or donkey from the manger and lead it to water? And this daughter of Abraham, who was bound by Satan, now eighteen years, did not have to be released from these bonds on Saturday? And while he spoke these things, all his opponents were ashamed; and all the people rejoiced in all that was gloriously done through him. <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Book II, Ch. 5 </h3>
<br/>
1. We cannot trust ourselves too much, as we often lack grace and discernment. We have little light and the light we have is quickly lost through our negligence. Often times, we don't even notice how blind we are inside. Often we do wrong, and even worse, we acquit ourselves. Sometimes we are carried away by passion and we think it is zeal. <br> </br>
We blame others for minor mistakes, we ignore much bigger ones at home. We feel and experience suffering inflicted on us by others quite easily, but we do not see what others suffer from us. Whoever judges himself rightly and fairly will not judge others harshly. <br> </br>
2. The inner man puts concern for his soul above all others; watching over himself diligently, he is easily silent about others. You will never become an inner person if you do not remain silent about others and take special care of yourself. Since you turn completely to God and the interior of your soul, you will hardly be touched by what you hear from outside. Where are you when you are not in yourself? But even if you run everything and find out, what have you gained by neglecting yourself? If you want to achieve peace and true union with God, you need to keep an eye on yourself first and foremost. <br> </br>
3. You will gain a lot by remaining free from all unnecessary temporal concerns. You will lose a lot if you love anything worldly. Let there be nothing great, nothing sublime, nothing dear and nice, except God himself and what comes from God. Consider all the comfort that creatures give. A soul loving God subjects everything to Him. God himself, eternal, immeasurable and filling everything with himself, is the only consolation of the soul and the true joy of the heart. <br> </br>
