<h3> St. Luke 17, 1-10 </h3>
<br/>
TIPS FOR STUDENTS.
<br/>
<br/>
And he said to his disciples, It is impossible that scandals should not come; but woe to him by whom they come. It would be better for him if a millstone were hung around his neck and thrown into the sea than for him to offend one of these little ones. Take care of yourself. If your brother sins against you, rebuke him; and if he regrets, forgive him. And if he should sin against you seven times a day, and turn to you seven times a day, saying, I am sorry; forgive him. <br> </br>
And the apostles said to the Lord, Increase our faith. And the Lord said, If ye have faith like a mustard seed, you will say to this mulberry tree, Root up, and go over the sea, and he will hear you. And which of you, having a plumber or a shepherd, when he returns from the field, will say to him: then you will eat and drink? Does he thank the servant for doing what he commanded? I do not think so. So also you, when you have done all that you are commanded, say: Servants are useless; what we ought to have done, we have done.

<h3> On following Christ, Book III, Ch. 30 </h3>
<br/>
1. Christ. Son, I am a defense in a day of distress (Cf. Nah 1: 7). Come to me whenever you feel bad. You delay the heavenly consolation the most by not taking too long to pray. For before you begin to plead with me, you first seek consolation and relief in external things. Everything will help you little, until you understand that I am the one who saves those who have trusted me, and that there is no effective help or useful advice or permanent remedy other than me. Now, after the storm, having regained peace of mind, strengthen your strength in the splendor of My mercy, because I am with you not only to renew everything completely, but to multiply your graces more generously and abundantly. <br> </br>
2. Is there anything impossible for me? Or will I be like one who speaks and does not do? Where is your faith? Be strong and persistent. Be patient and brave, you will be comforted in your time. Wait me, wait; I will come and heal you. What troubles you is only temptation, and what troubles you is vain fear. Why worry about an uncertain future? Probably to increase sorrow with sorrow. The day has enough of its misery. (Mt 6:34). <br> </br>
3. Unfortunately, human weakness is a weakness to deceive ourselves with such thoughts, and at the same time a sign of a still weak spirit to be easily deceived by the enemy's prompts. The enemy does not care if he deceives and deceives with truth or falsehood, or because he loves what we have today, or because he is afraid of what may happen. So do not let your heart be troubled or afraid. Trust in me and trust in my mercy. When you think you are far away from me, most of the time I am with you. When you think all is lost, often this is an opportunity for greater merit. Not everything is lost if something takes a turn other than what you want. Do not judge by momentary impressions or succumb to distress so much as if all hope of salvation for you has died out. <br> </br>
4. Do not think that you are completely abandoned when I send you some trouble or receive longed-for consolation; for this is how you come to the kingdom of heaven. And the fact that you experience adversities is undoubtedly better and more profitable for you than if you had everything according to your will. I know your innermost thoughts, and I know that it is very necessary for your salvation that you feel dry at times, lest success accidentally make you vanity, and that by self-delight in yourself you may not consider yourself as what you really are. What I gave, I can pick up and return when I like. <br> </br>
