<h3> St. Luke 18, 15-30 </h3>
<br/>
JESUS AND CHILDREN.
<br/>
<br/>
They brought him also little children, that he might touch them; and when the disciples saw them, they rebuked them. But Jesus, calling them, said, Suffer little children to come to me, and forbid them not; for of such is the kingdom of God. Truly I say to you, whoever does not accept the kingdom of God as a child will not enter it. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
RICH YOUTH.
<br/>
<br/>
And a certain prince asked him, saying, Good teacher, what will I receive eternal life by doing? Jesus said to him, Why do you call me good? No one is good but God Himself. You know the commandments: “You shall not kill; do not commit adultery; do not steal; do not give false testimony; honor your father and mother. " And he said, All these things I have kept from my youth. When Jesus heard this, he said to him, There is still one thing for you; sell everything you have and give to the poor, and you will have treasure in heaven, and come, follow me. When he heard this, he was sad because he was very rich. And when Jesus saw him grieved, he said, How difficult it is for those who have money to enter the kingdom of God! For it is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for a rich man to enter the kingdom of God. And those who heard said, Who then can be saved? He saith to them, What is impossible with men is possible with God. <br> </br>
And Peter said, Behold, we have forsaken everything, and followed you. And he said to me, Truly I say to you, there is no one who has left home, or parents or brothers, or wife or children, for the kingdom of God, and he would not receive much more in this time and in the next age of eternal life. <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Book III, Ch. 47 </h3>
<br/>
1. Christ. My son, do not be broken by the hardships You have accepted for me, and do not be disheartened by afflictions; in all cases, let my promise comfort you and comfort you. I have enough power to reward you beyond measure and measure. Soon you will be toiling, and you will not always be overwhelmed by suffering. Wait a little, and soon you will see the end of misery. The hour will come when all toil and anxiety will cease. Everything that passes with time is a small matter and lasts a short time. <br> </br>
2. Do what you ought to do, work faithfully in my vineyard, I will be your reward. Write, read, sing, sigh, be silent, pray, bravely endure adversities. Eternal life worth all this and even greater fights. On a day known to the Lord, there will be peace; there will then be no day or night of our time, but eternal light, infinite brightness, enduring peace, and safe rest. Then you will not say: Who will save me from the body of this death? (Rom 7:24). Neither shall you cry out: Woe is me that my pilgrimage is prolonged. (Ps 119: 5). For: He will put away death forever; and the Lord God will take away a tear from every face. (Is 25: 8), so all anxiety will disappear, eternal salvation will begin, a communion of glory and happiness, blessed joy. <br> </br>
3. Oh, if you only knew how much veneration the saints in heaven enjoy, how much glory shines upon those despised by the world and considered almost unworthy of life itself! Surely you would humble yourself to the very earth, and you would rather be subject to all than have power over just one person. You would not wish for a lifetime of carelessness in this life, but you would rejoice in the afflictions endured for God, and you would consider it a profit that people don't care about you. <br> </br>
4. Oh, if you had a taste of it and were concerned about it, would you dare to complain about anything at least once? Is it not worth all hardship for the sake of eternal life? It is not a small matter to lose or gain the kingdom of God! So lift your eyes to heaven! Here I am, and with me all my saints who have fought hard in this world; now they rejoice, now they are comforted, they are safe, they already have peace and they will stay with me forever in my Father's kingdom. <br> </br>
