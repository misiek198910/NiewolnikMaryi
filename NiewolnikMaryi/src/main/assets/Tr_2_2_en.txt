<h3> Treatise on the perfect devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary; Part III, 2 </h3>
<br/>
We turn to a brief presentation of the true devotion to the Blessed Virgin, which should be: 1 - internal, 2 - tender, 3 - holy, 4 - constant, 5 - selfless. <br> </br>
True devotion to the Blessed Virgin is internal, that is, it flows from the mind and heart, comes from deep respect for the Blessed Virgin, from a great image of her greatness, and from love for her. <br> </br>
It is tender, i.e. full of trust in the Blessed Virgin Mary, and the child's trust in the best mother. This devotion causes the soul to resort to her in all the needs of the body and soul with great simplicity, trust and tenderness, that she begs this good Mother for help always, everywhere and in every need: in doubts about light, in the wilderness to show the right path , in temptations to help, in weakness for strength, in falls for rebirth, in discouragement for reassurance, in scruples for advice, in crosses, works and life's adversities for consolation. In a word, in all ailments of body and soul, Mary is their ordinary refuge, and without fear of imposing too much on this good Mother or exposing herself to Jesus. <br> </br>
True devotion is sacred, i.e. it leads the soul to avoid sin and to imitate the virtues of the Blessed Virgin Mary, especially her profound humility, living faith, blind obedience, constant prayer, all-round mortification, divine purity, her ardent mercy, her heroic patience, her angelic sweetness and truly divine wisdom. These are the ten main virtues of the Virgin Mary. <br> </br>
True devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary Virgin Mary is permanent. It strengthens the soul in good and prevents a person from abandoning religious practices for trivial reasons. It makes the soul courageous to the world and its principles, courageous in fighting the flesh and its passions, and Satan and its temptations. Hence comes that the man who Blessed. He really serves the Virgin Mary, he is not fickle, harsh, restless or timid. This does not mean that such a soul does not fall at times or change its religious practices; but when he falls, he immediately rises, stretching out his hands to his good Mother; and even if she is haunted by dryness, for which she finds no consolation in the service, she does not become distracted, because she lives by faith (Jew 10:38) in Jesus and Mary and not by carnal feelings. <br> </br>
True devotion is disinterested, that is, it causes the soul not to look for itself, but only for God in the Holy Mother. The true worshiper of Mary does not serve this distinguished Queen for any benefit or gain, or for his own good, temporal or eternal, corporeal or spiritual, but only because it is worth serving her and, through her, God, he does not love Mary because she benefits him or expect some good from her, but because she is worthy of love. And that is why he loves her and serves her as much in anxiety and dryness as in the joy of the soul and in fervent worship; he loves her as much at Calvary as at the wedding at Cana in Galilee. How kind and dear in the eyes of God and the Blessed Virgin is one who does not look for himself in the services that
gives back! Unfortunately, how rarely do we meet such a soul! And that is why I picked up a pen to write down what I have taught for many years, publicly and privately. <br> </br>

<h3>Prayer to Mary</h3>
<b>St. Ludwik Maria Grignon de Montfort, translated by Magdalena Tuszyńska from an English text</b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail Mary, beloved daughter of the Eternal Father! Hail Mary, glorified Mother of the Son!
Hail Mary, faithful Bride of the Holy Spirit!
Hail Mary, my beloved Mother, beloved Lord, my all-powerful Queen!
Hail my joy, my glory, my heart and my soul!
You are all mine thanks to mercy, and I am all thanks to justice.
But I'm not completely yours yet. So now I devote myself to You completely, keeping nothing for myself or for others. And if you still see something in me that doesn't belong to you, I am begging you, remove it,
and make Yourself the absolute ruler of all that is mine.
Destroy in me everything that can hurt God, erode and eliminate
plant in me and cultivate everything that pleases You.
Let the light of faith disperse the darkness of my mind, let deep humility take the place of my pride, let high reflection enter the place of my wandering imagination, let the image of God fill my memory with His presence, let the fiery love of your heart ignite my coldness, let Your virtues take the place my sins, may Your merits be the only decoration in God's eyes and give me everything that is expected of me.
Please, beloved Mother, if it is possible, that I would have no other spirit than yours, that I would know Jesus and His God's Will; that I would not have a soul other than yours to worship and praise God, that I would not have a heart other than yours, to love God with a love as pure and burning as yours.
I am not asking you for visions, revelations, special devotions or spiritual satisfaction.
Your privilege is that you see God very clearly
Your privilege is to enjoy heavenly pleasure,
Your privilege is to triumph gloriously in Heaven at the Son's right hand
And to wield absolute power over Angels, people and demons.
Your privilege is to dispose of all God's gifts as you wish.
This, heavenly Mary, is the "best part" that the Lord has given you and which will never be taken away from You. And this thought fills my heart with joy.
And as for my part here on earth, I am asking for nothing but what is yours: to believe deeply without spiritual joys, to suffer joyfully without the comfort of people,
to die constantly for yourself, without a respite, and to work earnestly and unselfishly until You die, as the most humble servant among Your servants.
The only grace for which I am begging you to receive for me is that I may, every day and in
every moment of my life he could say:
Amen, let it be so - in front of all that you did while you were still on earth; Amen, let it be so - in front of all that you do now in Heaven;
Amen, so be it - in front of all that you do in my soul,
that you alone could glorify Jesus in me, in time and in eternity. Amen.
