<h3> Treatise on the perfect devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary; Part V, </h3>
<br/>
This devotion is a path: easy, short, perfect and sure, leading to union with Our Lord, which is Christian perfection. <br> </br>
This devotion is an easy path that Jesus Christ Himself has paved in coming to us, and on which there is no obstacle to reach Him. It is possible to achieve union with God in a different way, but then it will not be without more numerous crosses, severe mortifications and many greater barriers, difficult to overcome. The soul then passes through dark nights, through battles and extraordinary sufferings, over cliffs, through a thorny path and terrible wilderness. On the other hand, the way of Mary is gentler and calmer. <br> </br>
This devotion is a short path (St. Bernard, Laudes gloriosae Virg.) That leads to Jesus Christ; once because it is impossible to get lost on it, and then because the path is more joyful and easier, since it is much faster. Living in surrender and depending on Mary, we will do more in a short time than living for years in lawlessness and relying on our own strength, because it is written that a man obedient and devoted to the Mother of God will signify the victory over all enemies. (Cf. Prov 21:28). It is true that his enemies will try to hinder his progress, force him to retreat or lead him to fall, but relying on Mary, with her help and under her guidance, he will not fall, will not fall back or even delay, but will be he followed the giant's footsteps towards Christ, the same path that, it is written, Jesus, the giant's footsteps, came to us in a short time. (Cf. Ps 18: 5). </br>
Devotion to the Blessed Virgin is the perfect way to come to Jesus Christ and be united with Him, because Holy. The Virgin Mary was the most perfect and holy Being of all creatures and because Jesus <br> </br>
Christ, coming to us in a perfect way, chose no other path in His great and marvelous journey. Highest and inconceivable, inaccessible, He Who is, wanted to come to us, the little earthworms who are nothing. How did it happen? The Most High has come down to us in a perfect and divine way through the humble Mary, without thereby losing any of His divinity and holiness; therefore, except through Mary, the little ones should ascend to the Most High in a perfect and divine way, fearing nothing.
Inconceivable, he allowed the little Mary to embrace him and completely embrace him, without losing any of her immutability, so we are to let this inconspicuous Virgin embrace us completely and lead us without any reservations. The inaccessible one came closer to us through Mary and through her he united himself most closely, and even personally, to our humanity, thus losing nothing of the splendor of His Majesty; therefore we too should draw near to God through Mary and unite ourselves most closely to His Majesty, not fearing rejection. "He That Is" wanted to come to what is not and make what is not become God and become "Who is." And he did it perfectly by giving himself up and surrendering himself completely to the young Virgin Mary, without ceasing in time to be "Who is," the Eternal. Likewise, even though we are nothing, we can, through Mary, become like God through grace and glory, without fear of being lost, that is, by giving ourselves so perfectly and completely that we are nothing in ourselves and everything in her.
The devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary presented here is a sure way to come to Jesus Christ, to be united with Him and thus achieve perfection, 1) because this practice is not new, rather it is so old that its beginning cannot be clearly marked. (St. Odilon, abbot of Cluny, who lived around 1040, was one of the first to introduce this devotion in France. Thanks to Father Stanisław Falacjusz, TJ, it experienced enormous development in Poland - King Władysław IV ordered the Jesuits to extend this devotion in France. the whole kingdom.) 2) This devotion is a sure means to come to Christ, for it is a property of the Most Holy. The Virgin Mary is to lead us on the sure path to Christ, just as it is the quality of Jesus to lead us safely to the Eternal Father.

<h3>Prayer to Mary</h3>
<b>St. Ludwik Maria Grignon de Montfort, translated by Magdalena Tuszyńska from an English text</b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail Mary, beloved daughter of the Eternal Father! Hail Mary, glorified Mother of the Son!
Hail Mary, faithful Bride of the Holy Spirit!
Hail Mary, my beloved Mother, beloved Lord, my all-powerful Queen!
Hail my joy, my glory, my heart and my soul!
You are all mine thanks to mercy, and I am all thanks to justice.
But I'm not completely yours yet. So now I devote myself to You completely, keeping nothing for myself or for others. And if you still see something in me that doesn't belong to you, I am begging you, remove it,
and make Yourself the absolute ruler of all that is mine.
Destroy in me everything that can hurt God, erode and eliminate
plant in me and cultivate everything that pleases You.
Let the light of faith disperse the darkness of my mind, let deep humility take the place of my pride, let high reflection enter the place of my wandering imagination, let the image of God fill my memory with His presence, let the fiery love of your heart ignite my coldness, let Your virtues take the place my sins, may Your merits be the only decoration in God's eyes and give me everything that is expected of me.
Please, beloved Mother, if it is possible, that I would have no other spirit than yours, that I would know Jesus and His God's Will; that I would not have a soul other than yours to worship and praise God, that I would not have a heart other than yours, to love God with a love as pure and burning as yours.
I am not asking you for visions, revelations, special devotions or spiritual satisfaction.
Your privilege is that you see God very clearly
Your privilege is to enjoy heavenly pleasure,
Your privilege is to triumph gloriously in Heaven at the Son's right hand
And to wield absolute power over Angels, people and demons.
Your privilege is to dispose of all God's gifts as you wish.
This, heavenly Mary, is the "best part" that the Lord has given you and which will never be taken away from You. And this thought fills my heart with joy.
And as for my part here on earth, I am asking for nothing but what is yours: to believe deeply without spiritual joys, to suffer joyfully without the comfort of people,
to die constantly for yourself, without a respite, and to work earnestly and unselfishly until You die, as the most humble servant among Your servants.
The only grace for which I am begging you to receive for me is that I may, every day and in
every moment of my life he could say:
Amen, let it be so - in front of all that you did while you were still on earth; Amen, let it be so - in front of all that you do now in Heaven;
Amen, so be it - in front of all that you do in my soul,
that you alone could glorify Jesus in me, in time and in eternity. Amen.
