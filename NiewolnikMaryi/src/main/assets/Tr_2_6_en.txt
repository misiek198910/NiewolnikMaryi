<h3>Treatise on Perfect Devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary; Part I, 2 </h3>

From the above it is clear that Mary received from God great power over chosen souls. Otherwise She could not have made Her home in them, as God the Father had commanded, She could not have shaped them, nourished them and, as a mother, given birth to eternal life, She could not have considered them as Her inheritance, She could not have shaped them in Jesus Christ, nor Jesus Christ in them, could not take root in their hearts of virtue and be the inseparable companion of the Holy Spirit. in all works of grace; Therefore, she could not do all this if she did not have the right and power over souls by the special grace of the Most High, who gives Her power over His only and natural Son, and also gave Her power over His adopted children, not only in the flesh, which would mean little. , but also about the soul.
Just as Jesus is by nature and through Redemption the King of heaven and earth, so Mary is Queen by grace. The Kingdom of Jesus Christ exists mainly in the heart or interior of man, according to the words of the Holy Scripture. The Kingdom of God is within you (Luke 17:21), therefore the Kingdom of the Blessed Virgin develops mainly in the interior of man, i.e. in his soul, and therefore she receives, together with Her Son, more glory in souls than in all visible creatures. Hence, together with the saints, we can call her: "Queen of All Hearts."
From the fact that the Most Holy The Virgin is needed by God, that is, she is needed conditionally, because this is God's Will, and the inescapable conclusion is that people need her more to achieve eternal salvation. ...Fathers of the Church such as: St. Augustine, Saint Cyril of Jerusalem, Saint Germanus, Saint John Damascene, Saint Anselm, Saint Bernard, Saint Bernardine, Saint Thomas, Saint Bonaventure proves conclusively that devotion to the Blessed Virgin is necessary for salvation. To prove this truth, I collected a long series of passages from the writings of the Holy Fathers. and Doctors. However, not wanting to prolong my arguments too much, I will stop at one: "To you, Holy Virgin, I commit myself, because you are the buckler of salvation that God gives to those he wants to save." (St. John Damascene)

<h3>Prayer to Mary</h3>
<b>St. Ludwik Maria Grignon de Montfort, translated by Magdalena Tuszyńska from the text in English</b>
<br/>
<br/>
Hail, Mary, beloved Daughter of the Eternal Father! Hail, Mary, glorified Mother of the Son!
Hail, Mary, faithful Spouse of the Holy Spirit!
Hail, Mary, my beloved Mother, my beloved Sovereign, my all-powerful Queen!
Hail, my joy, my glory, my heart and my soul!
You are all mine through mercy, and I am all yours through justice.
But I'm not completely yours yet. So now I surrender myself completely to You, keeping nothing for myself or for others. And if you still see something in me that doesn't belong to you, I beg you to remove it,
and make Yourself the absolute ruler of all that is mine.
Destroy everything in me that can cause displeasure to God, root it out and eliminate it,
and plant in me and nurture everything that will please you.
Let the light of faith dispel the darkness of my mind, let deep humility take the place of my pride, let sublime meditation take the place of my wandering imagination, let the image of God constantly fill my memory with its presence, let the fiery love of your heart kindle the coldness of my heart, let your virtues take the place my sins, let Your merits be the only decoration in the eyes of God and prepare in me everything that is expected of me.
Please, dearly beloved Mother, if it is possible, let me have no other spirit than Yours, so that I can know Jesus and His Divine Will; so that I have no other soul than yours to worship and glorify God, so that I have no other heart than yours, to love God with a love as pure and flaming as yours.
I am not asking You for visions, revelations, special devotions, or spiritual satisfactions.
It is your privilege to see God very clearly,
It is your privilege to enjoy heavenly delight,
It is your privilege to triumph gloriously in Heaven at the right hand of the Son
And hold in your hands absolute power over Angels, people and demons.
It is your privilege to dispose of all God's gifts as you wish.
This, O heavenly Mary, is the "best part" that the Lord has given You, and which will never be taken away from You. And this thought fills my heart with joy.
And as for my part here on earth, I ask for nothing else than what is Yours: to believe deeply without spiritual joys, to suffer joyfully without the consolation of men,
to die continually to myself, without a moment's respite, and to work diligently and unselfishly for You until death, as the most humble servant among Your servants.
The only grace I am begging you to receive for me is that every day and every day
every moment of my life he could say:
Amen, so be it, for all that you did while you were still on earth; Amen, let it be so - for all that you are doing now in Heaven;
Amen, let it be so - for all that you do in my soul,
so that You Yourself can glorify Jesus in me, in time and eternity. Amen.
