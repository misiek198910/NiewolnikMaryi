<h3> On following Christ, Book II, Ch. 7 </h3>
<br/>
1. Blessed is he who understands what it means to love Jesus and to deny himself for Jesus. For this Beloved one must leave everything dear to us, because Jesus wants to be loved above all else. The love of creation is deceptive and unstable; Jesus' love was persistent and faithful. Whoever becomes attached to creatures will fall with those who fall; whoever embraces Jesus firmly will be established forever. Love Jesus and make you a friend, even if they all depart you; He will not abandon you and will not let you die. Whether you like it or not, you will have to part ways with everyone. <br> </br>
2. While living and dying, hold on to Jesus and entrust yourself to his fidelity; He alone can help you when they all leave you. Your beloved wants all of you: he wants to possess your heart himself and to have his royal throne in it. If you could free yourself from creatures, Jesus would be happy to live with you. You will lose almost everything you base on people, not Jesus. Do not put your trust in a shaky reed, nor lean on it, for all flesh is grass and all its glory is like the flower of the field. The grass withered and the flower fell. (Is 40: 6-7) <br> </br>
3. You will be easily disappointed if you pay attention to outward appearances. If you are looking for consolation and self-benefit in others, you will likely suffer harm more often. If you are looking for Jesus in everything, you will surely find Jesus. In looking for yourself, you will find yourself, but to your doom. A man who does not look for Jesus harms himself more than the whole world and all its enemies harm him. <br> </br>

<h3> On following Christ, Book II, Ch. 11 </h3>
<br/>
1. Jesus now has many lovers of his heavenly kingdom, but few who would like to bear his cross. He has many who want consolation, few who are ready to suffer. He finds numerous participants at the table, but little ready for fasting. Everyone wants to be happy with Him, but hardly anyone suffers. Many follow Jesus even to the breaking of the bread, but few to the point of tilting the cup of the passion. Many admire His miracles, hardly anyone wants to share with Him the insult of the cross. Many love Him until they face adversity. Many praise and bless him as long as he comforts them. If Jesus hides himself and leaves them alone for even a moment, they either complain or lose their spirits completely. <br> </br>
2. Those who love Jesus for Jesus, and not for any of their own consolation, bless Him both in all affliction and tribulation of the soul and in the midst of the greatest joy. And even if he would never deign to please them, they would always praise Him in the same way and would like to give Him thanks. <br> </br>
3. How much pure love of Jesus can, free from personal gain or self-love! Should we not call all those who are always looking for consolations mercenaries? Are they not more lovers of themselves than those of Christ who constantly think about their comforts and benefits? Will there be anyone who would like to serve God selflessly? <br> </br>
4. It is rare to come across such a spiritual man who would empty himself of everything. For who can find the truly poor in spirit, free from attachment to created things? Far away and from the uttermost limits is his price. (Cf. Prov 31:10). <br> </br>

<h3>Prayer to Jesus Christ</h3>
<b>St. Ludwik Maria Grignon de Montfort - translated by Magdalena Tuszyńska, from an English text</b>
<br/>
<br/>
O beloved Jesus, allow me to bow deeply to You, thank you for the grace You have given me, giving me Your Holy Mother through the service of Holy Slavery.
Thanks to this, she can be my lawyer before Your Majesty and support in my infinite misery. OH lord I am so devastated that without this beloved Mother I would inevitably be lost.
Yes, I need Mary by your side and all around me: that she would alleviate your righteous anger,
because I have been against you so often; that she would save me from the eternal punishment of Your righteousness which I owe rightly; so that she could ponder you, address you
to pray to you and to appease you; that she could help me save my soul and the souls of others; In a word, I need Mary, so that I can always do Your Holy Will and strive for Your greatest glory in everything.
Ah, if I could spread the mercy you have shown to me all over the world!
That everyone would know that if it wasn't for Mary, I would be condemned to perpetual damnation!
If I could offer the due thank you for such a great blessing!
Mary is in me. Oh, what a wealth! Oh what a relief!
And should I not belong to her completely? Oh, what a lack of gratitude!
My beloved Savior, send me death rather than such ingratitude,
because I should die rather than live without completely belonging to Mary.
At the foot of the Cross with Saint. John the Evangelist, I accepted her a thousand times as my own mother,
and I gave her to her so many times; but if I have not done it the way you wish, beloved Jesus, I am now resuming this sacrifice in the way that You would have wanted it. And if you see anything in my soul or in my body that doesn't belong to this mighty Queen, I pray to You,
that you remove it and reject it far, because everything that is inside me and does not belong to Mary is unworthy of You.
O Holy Spirit, grant me all these favors. Plant a Tree of real life in my soul, which is Mary; cultivate and care for it so that it can grow, cover with flowers,
and bring fruit of life in abundance.
O Holy Spirit, give me a deep devotion to Mary, Your faithful Bride,
give me great trust in her motherly heart and a safe refuge in her mercy, so that thanks to her you can truly shape Jesus Christ in me,
great and mighty, until his time was completely filled. Amen.
