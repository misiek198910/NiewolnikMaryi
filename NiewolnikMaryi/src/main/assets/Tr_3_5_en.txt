<h3> On following Christ, Book IV, Ch. 1, 1-2; 8. </h3>
<br/>
<h3> Chap. 1, 1. </h3>
<br/>
These are your words, O Christ, the eternal truth. Though not renounced at one time and not saved in one place. Because they are yours and true, I should accept them all with gratitude and faith. Yours are. You have spoken them, but they are mine at the same time, for you have renounced them for my salvation. I accept them with joy from Your lips, asking them to sink into my heart as soon as possible. I am moved by these words of great goodness, full of sweetness and love, but my sins and my impure conscience are troubling me from participating in such a great mystery. The sweetness of your words encourages me, but I am troubled by many offenses.
<br/>
<br/>
2. You command me to come to You confidently if they wish to have a part with You, and that I may eat the food of immortality if I wish to have eternal life and glory. You say: Come to me, all you who work and
you are loaded and I will keep you cool. (Mt 11:28). O sweet and friendly words to the ears of a sinner, that you, Lord, my God, the wretched and the poor, are inviting you to eat your most holy Body! But who am I, Lord, that I would dare come to You? For if the heavens and the heavens cannot embrace you (3 Kings 8:27) and you say: come to me, all of you ...
<br/>
<br/>
<h3> Chap. 8, 1. </h3>
<br/>
The voice of Christ, as I, with my arms spread on the cross, with my body bared, offered myself freely to God the Father for your sins, so that there was nothing left in me that I would not sacrifice to atone for God's wrath, so you should voluntarily, every day at Holy Mass. give yourself to Him as a pure and holy sacrifice, body and soul, with all your strength and feelings. I am asking nothing more of you than that you should try to give yourself to Me. Whatever you give to me without giving yourself, I have nothing for nothing, because I am not looking for your gifts, but for yourself.
<br/>
<br/>
2. As it would not be enough for you to have everything but me, no sacrifice of yours can please me if you do not give me your whole self. Offer yourself to me, give yourself to me, and your sacrifice will be accepted. Behold, I have offered myself to the Father for you; I have given you all my body and all my blood to eat, that I may be all yours and you may be mine for ever. If you lean on yourself and do not voluntarily submit to My will, your sacrifice will not be complete and our union incomplete. A voluntary submission of yourself to God should precede all your affairs; then you will achieve freedom and grace. There are few people who are internally enlightened and free, because hardly anyone can completely deny himself. And my judgment is firm: none of you who do not abandon everything he has can be my disciple. (Luke 14:33). So if you want to be my disciple, give me yourself and all your feelings.

<h3>Prayer to Jesus Christ</h3>
<br/>
<b>St. Ludwik Maria Grignon de Montfort - translated by Magdalena Tuszyńska, from an English text</b>
O beloved Jesus, allow me to bow deeply to You, thank you for the grace You have given me, giving me Your Holy Mother through the service of Holy Slavery.
Thanks to this, she can be my lawyer before Your Majesty and support in my infinite misery. OH lord I am so devastated that without this beloved Mother I would inevitably be lost.
Yes, I need Mary by your side and all around me: that she would alleviate your righteous anger,
because I have been against you so often; that she would save me from the eternal punishment of Your righteousness which I owe rightly; so that she could ponder you, address you
to pray to you and to appease you; that she could help me save my soul and the souls of others; In a word, I need Mary, so that I can always do Your Holy Will and strive for Your greatest glory in everything.
Ah, if I could spread the mercy you have shown to me all over the world!
That everyone would know that if it wasn't for Mary, I would be condemned to perpetual damnation!
If I could offer the due thank you for such a great blessing!
Mary is in me. Oh, what a wealth! Oh what a relief!
And should I not belong to her completely? Oh, what a lack of gratitude!
My beloved Savior, send me death rather than such ingratitude,
because I should die rather than live without completely belonging to Mary.
At the foot of the Cross with Saint. John the Evangelist, I accepted her a thousand times as my own mother,
and I gave her to her so many times; but if I have not done it the way you wish, beloved Jesus, I am now resuming this sacrifice in the way that You would have wanted it. And if you see anything in my soul or in my body that doesn't belong to this mighty Queen, I pray to You,
that you remove it and reject it far, because everything that is inside me and does not belong to Mary is unworthy of You.
O Holy Spirit, grant me all these favors. Plant a Tree of real life in my soul, which is Mary; cultivate and care for it so that it can grow, cover with flowers,
and bring fruit of life in abundance.
O Holy Spirit, give me a deep devotion to Mary, Your faithful Bride,
give me great trust in her motherly heart and a safe refuge in her mercy, so that thanks to her you can truly shape Jesus Christ in me,
great and mighty, until his time was completely filled. Amen.
