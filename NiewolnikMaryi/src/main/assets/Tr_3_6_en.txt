<h3> Treatise on the Perfect Devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary, Part IV </h3>
<br/>
A great example of all the truths about the Blessed Virgin and her children and servants is given to us by the Holy Spirit in the Scriptures, namely in the story of Jacob (Gen. 37), who received the blessing of Father Isaac through the efforts and efforts of his mother Rebecca.
Mary, having gathered favors for her children and faithful servants, after receiving the blessing of the Heavenly Father for them, in union with Jesus Christ, secures them in Jesus and Jesus in them. He stays with them, constantly watches over them, for fear that they would not lose God's favors and fall into the clutches of their enemies. “He keeps the saints in all their fullness” (St. Bonaventure, Cf. 174), helps them to survive to the end. This is the most appropriate explanation of the story of Jacob and Esau, a story from long ago about the elect and the damned, a history so unknown and so full of mysteries.

<h3> On following Christ, Book IV, Ch. 13. </h3>
<br/>
1. Student's voice. Who will help me, Lord, that I may find You Himself, open my whole heart to You and be glad in You, as my soul desires, and that no one will despise me anymore. (Song 8: 1). Let no creation occupy me and touch me, but you yourself speak to me, and I speak to you, as the beloved speaks with the beloved and as a friend communicates with a friend. I am begging you for this, this is what I desire, that I may unite myself with You and detach my heart from all created things, and through frequent Mass and Holy Communion. he learned to value the goods of heaven more.
Ah, my Lord and God, when will I be completely united to You, completely absorbed by You, that I may finally forget about myself? You in me and I in you: let me remain in this union forever. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
2. Truly, You are my Lover, chosen from thousands (Song 5:10), it is in You that my soul lives for all the days of my life. Truly, You are my consolation, in You undisturbed peace and true rest. Apart from you, toil, pain and endless poverty. Indeed, you are the hidden God (Is 45:15), you abhor the perverse, and his conversation with simple things. (Prov 3:32). O Lord, how good and sweet is your spirit in everything! (Wisdom 12.1). To show Your goodness to your sons, You deign to nourish them with the sweetest bread that came down from Heaven. And you have no other nation
so great who would have gods as close to him as the Lord our God, is present to all our prayers. (Deut 4: 7) Near is all his faithful, giving them Myself to eat for their daily comfort and to raise their hearts. <br> </br>
<br/>
<br/>
3. What people are as happy as Christ's? What creatures as beloved as a godly man whom God Himself comes to nourish with His glorious Body? Inexpressible grace, wonderful goodness, inexhaustible love that God showed to man! But what will I give to the Lord for such a gift, for such immeasurable love? I cannot offer anything nicer to my God than uniting my whole heart with Him. And when the soul has a perfect fusion with Him, then the heart will beat joyfully. Then the Lord will say to me, If you want to be with me, I want to be with you. And I will answer him: Lord, please stay with me, because I want to be with you very much! I only desire one thing, that my heart should be united to You.

<h3>Prayer to Jesus Christ</h3>
<b>St. Ludwik Maria Grignon de Montfort - translated by Magdalena Tuszyńska, from an English text</b>
<br/>
<br/>
O beloved Jesus, allow me to bow deeply to You, thank you for the grace You have given me, giving me Your Holy Mother through the service of Holy Slavery.
Thanks to this, she can be my lawyer before Your Majesty and support in my infinite misery. OH lord I am so devastated that without this beloved Mother I would inevitably be lost.
Yes, I need Mary by your side and all around me: that she would alleviate your righteous anger,
because I have been against you so often; that she would save me from the eternal punishment of Your righteousness which I owe rightly; so that she could ponder you, address you
to pray to you and to appease you; that she could help me save my soul and the souls of others; In a word, I need Mary, so that I can always do Your Holy Will and strive for Your greatest glory in everything.
Ah, if I could spread the mercy you have shown to me all over the world!
That everyone would know that if it wasn't for Mary, I would be condemned to perpetual damnation!
If I could offer the due thank you for such a great blessing!
Mary is in me. Oh, what a wealth! Oh what a relief!
And should I not belong to her completely? Oh, what a lack of gratitude!
My beloved Savior, send me death rather than such ingratitude,
because I should die rather than live without completely belonging to Mary.
At the foot of the Cross with Saint. John the Evangelist, I accepted her a thousand times as my own mother,
and I gave her to her so many times; but if I have not done it the way you wish, beloved Jesus, I am now resuming this sacrifice in the way that You would have wanted it. And if you see anything in my soul or in my body that doesn't belong to this mighty Queen, I pray to You,
that you remove it and reject it far, because everything that is inside me and does not belong to Mary is unworthy of You.
O Holy Spirit, grant me all these favors. Plant a Tree of real life in my soul, which is Mary; cultivate and care for it so that it can grow, cover with flowers,
and bring fruit of life in abundance.
O Holy Spirit, give me a deep devotion to Mary, Your faithful Bride,
give me great trust in her motherly heart and a safe refuge in her mercy, so that thanks to her you can truly shape Jesus Christ in me,
great and mighty, until his time was completely filled. Amen.
