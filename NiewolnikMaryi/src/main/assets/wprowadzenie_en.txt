In English, the translation of the "Treaty ..." from French was made in 1862 by Father Frederick
Faber. Father Faber, following the recommendations of St. Louis about the need to prepare before refusing the final
The Act of Offering, developed an additional set of exercises for a 33-day preparation period, gave readings on
each day, using passages from the Holy Bible and other books, as well as quoting prayers on

constituting an almost integral part of the book, which is completely unknown in Poland. <br> <br/>
The 33-day preparation period is usually carried out by a priest - for a group of people, often for the entire parish. Including

<b> Preparation for the Act of Offering - Fr. Fryderyk Wilhelm Faber, D.D. </ B>
In the introduction to the book in English "A treatise on a true devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary", the words of Saint. Ludwika Grignon de Montfort, Ch. IV. Par. 227, where he says: "Those who want to devote themselves to this special service, which is not yet a brotherhood, even if it was very desirable (wish St. Louis came true) - should work for at least twelve days to get rid of the spirit this world, which is an opponent of the spirit of Jesus Christ; they should continue to devote three weeks to be filled with Jesus Christ through the Blessed Virgin ... "
Father Fryderyk Faber, DD, writes: "Ludwik de Montfort recommends that we prepare for the Act of Sacrifice by means of spiritual exercises, which are actually not obligatory, but make it possible for us to better absorb this act due to the dispositions that form in our souls. We divide these exercises into two periods: <br> <br/>
<br> <u> <b> Period one: </b> </u> twelve days during which we try to "free ourselves from the spirit of the world" <br/>
<br> <u> <b> Second period: </b> </u> divided into three one-week episodes: <br/>
<b> First week </b> - Getting to know yourself, <br/>
<b> Second week </b> - Getting to know the Blessed Virgin Mary, <br/>
<b> Third week </b> - Getting to know the Jesus Christ. <br/>
<br> These periods determined by Saint. Louis de Montfort do not close themselves in strict and unchangeable borders. They can be extended or shortened, e.g. a period of three days is usually sufficient for the annual renewal of the Act of Offering. <br/>
